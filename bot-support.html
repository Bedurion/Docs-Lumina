Give every request a private, responsible home.

Members choose a support category, answer its required questions and receive a private Discord thread shared only with the right participants and staff.

Open Support guideSupport overview

In plain English

The public channel stays clean.

The support channel contains one permanent panel. When a member opens a ticket, the conversation happens in a private thread instead of posting a visible “thread created” message for everyone.

Each category can ask different required questions, require media, limit which users can see it and require both a global moderator role and one or more specialist support roles.

Ticket process

Responsibility remains visible from opening to archive.

Open

The creator submits category-specific information and optional required media.

Claim

One staff member can take responsibility while administrators retain oversight.

Resolve

Staff or the creator closes the active conversation immediately.

Archive

Discord locks and archives the private thread with messages and attachments intact.

After closure

History remains findable without building a transcript store.

My Tickets

Members see their own tickets; staff see tickets in which they participated, with paginated navigation.

Private participants

Authorized staff can add or remove another person when the case genuinely needs them.

Satisfaction

The creator can mark the result satisfactory or not; an ignored request resolves after the configured period.

Discord archive

Conversation text and attachments remain in the archived thread rather than duplicating media in Luminox storage.
