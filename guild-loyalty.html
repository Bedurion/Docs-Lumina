Lumina Loyalty turns verified participation into one transparent account progression. It helps the guild recognize the members who contribute, return and keep community life moving.

Understand LoyaltySee the Luminox system

Contribution with context

A number should explain trust, not replace it.

Loyalty Points record eligible actions such as helping with events, supporting members, strengthening the guild and contributing through configured systems. The total belongs to the Discord account, so changing a main character does not erase the person behind the work.

The result is a visible Loyalty Level that complements human judgment. It never turns leadership, friendship or respect into an automatic score.

How it works

Earned through real guild participation.

Every reward follows a defined source, eligibility rule and permanent account history.

01

Participate

Eligible activity can include events, support, GuildBank work, recruitment, boosts, streaming and other configured contributions.

02

Verify

Luminox connects the action to a registered account and records the reason instead of silently changing a balance.

03

Progress

Points accumulate into Loyalty Levels that remain visible in member identity and private account views.

04

Recognize

Higher levels communicate sustained commitment and can support distinctions, access or functions where Lumina explicitly configures them.

One progression, many routes

There is no single correct way to help.

A member may organize activities, answer Support tickets, contribute resources, welcome recruits or support the Discord server. Loyalty brings those different routes into one understandable account history.

That flexibility matters: recognition should not only reward the loudest member or the person with the most available playtime.

Why the history matters

Transparent rewards are harder to manipulate.

Account-linked

Characters can change while progression remains connected to the verified Discord account.

Reviewable

Important rewards and corrections explain their source in permanent history rather than appearing as unexplained totals.

Retention-aware

Conditional rewards can remain partially at risk and settle over time instead of encouraging short-term abuse.

Visible, not absolute

Levels support recognition, but staff decisions still require context, responsibility and judgment.
