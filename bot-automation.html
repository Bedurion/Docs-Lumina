In plain English

Current information refreshes; permanent history does not.

A public panel is the latest view of a system, so Luminox edits or safely repositions it when meaningful data changes. A permanent log is historical evidence, so old entries remain fixed.

Temporary responses follow a different lifecycle. Quick confirmations disappear rapidly; readable profiles last longer; registration codes remain available long enough to complete verification.

Different jobs, different timing

Automation follows the cost and value of the data.

Panel refresh

Current views update after relevant changes, not simply because the bot restarted.

Channel autodelete

Administrators choose channels, intervals and whether old existing messages should also be cleaned.

Private response cleanup

Interaction detail is deleted according to how long a member realistically needs it.

Metadata retention

Operational metadata follows edition-aware cleanup rules while Discord keeps archived conversations.
