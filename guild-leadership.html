Guild · Leadership

Authority should make the guild easier to trust.

Lumina separates broad leadership from specialist responsibility so members know where to ask, staff know what they own and important decisions leave a clear record.

A visible responsibility map

Different roles. One standard.

Leadership should not make members navigate a hierarchy for every question. Each responsibility has a clear owner, while private Support remains available whenever a member is unsure where to ask.

01

Direction

Leader and Vice Leaders

Protect the long-term direction of Lumina, resolve the most important decisions and coordinate staff responsibility.

02

Moderation

Trusted staff

Support members, review sensitive workflows and act where the guild has granted clear authority.

03

Specialists

Purpose-specific roles

Recruitment, treasury, support and other systems can require an additional role so access matches responsibility.

04

Members

The community itself

Members create the activities, reports, applications and feedback that give leadership something real to support.

How responsibility should feel

Clear enough to use. Human enough to approach.

Listen

Start with context

A decision should understand the people, evidence and history involved instead of reacting to one isolated message.

Explain

Make outcomes understandable

Members should know what happened, what standard applied and what next step is available when appropriate.

Record

Keep important actions traceable

Discord logs and private threads preserve decisions without turning sensitive information into public spectacle.

Review

Allow correction

Structured claims, disputes and staff workflows create room to revisit a decision when better information appears.

When a member needs help

Use the route that protects the conversation.

General questions can begin in the appropriate community channel. Registration problems, complaints, evidence and personal situations belong in the private Support system, where the responsible staff can answer without exposing the member.
