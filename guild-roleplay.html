Why we roleplay

A different way to share Tibia.

Progression is only one part of the game. Roleplay gives us time to slow down, notice the world around our characters and build stories that belong to the group rather than to a leaderboard.

Sessions can be light and social or develop into longer chronicles. The goal is never perfect acting. It is to create a space where members can contribute ideas, react to one another and enjoy a memorable experience together.

Original charactersBring a new persona or deepen the character you already play. Shared directionThe story grows through decisions instead of following one fixed script. Guild memoriesImportant scenes can become chronicles in the Lumina Gallery.

Choose your place

Participation has more than one form.

No previous roleplay experience is required. Begin at the level that feels comfortable.

✦

Player

Live the story

Play a character, respond to the scene and help other participants create moments together.

  • Prepare a simple motivation
  • Listen and leave space for others
  • Accept that outcomes may be unexpected

Narrator

Open the world

Introduce a situation, guide its pace and adapt the experience to the choices made by the players.

  • Set expectations before starting
  • Offer choices instead of one solution
  • Protect the agreed tone and boundaries
◉

Observer

Watch before joining

Follow a session respectfully, understand how the group plays and join a future story when ready.

  • No pressure to perform immediately
  • Learn the rhythm of a session
  • Respect immersion while observing

Roleplay standards

Creativity works when everyone feels included.

Our guild rules continue to apply during roleplay. Fiction never excuses disrespect, pressure or behavior that makes another member uncomfortable.

01

Consent before intensity

Agree on tone and sensitive themes before the session. Any participant can ask to pause or redirect a scene.

02

Separate character and player

Conflict between characters is not permission for conflict between members. Keep in-character and out-of-character communication clear.

03

Share the spotlight

Strong sessions give every participant room to make decisions, speak and influence what happens.

04

Respect the schedule

Arrive prepared, communicate delays and help the narrator conclude at the time agreed by the group.
