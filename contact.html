Help us help you

Send the context that changes the answer.

You do not need to diagnose the problem before contacting us. A short, precise message is enough: explain the outcome you need, include what you already tried and leave out private credentials.

Planning Luminox?

Share your guild size, current pain points and the two or three features you would introduce first.

Reporting a problem?

Share the server, channel, panel or button, approximate time, expected result and visible error.

Joining Lumina?

Share your main character, world, what you are looking for and any question about our rules or process.

Your credentials stay yours

Never send secrets to support.

A normal Luminox conversation never requires your bot token, environment file, raw database or Discord password.

Private when it should be

Your evidence does not need to become a public conversation.

When a question includes member information, screenshots or configuration details, Luminox moves it into a private Discord thread. The requester, responsible staff, invited participants and server administrators can continue there without exposing the case to the channel.

  • Required questions keep the first message useful.
  • Images and replies remain in their original order.
  • Resolved conversations are locked and archived in Discord.

What you can expect

A useful answer, not another layer of complexity.

Our objective is to help you choose, configure or resolve the next concrete step. We explain limitations honestly and do not push a larger plan when a smaller one solves the real need.

Plain language

No technical vocabulary required.

Describe the goal in your own words. We will translate it into the relevant Luminox system or Lumina process.

Honest fit

Free is recommended when Free is enough.

Product guidance begins with the smallest useful setup, the correct edition and the limits that matter to your server.

Clear ownership

You will know who should act next.

The reply separates what you can change, what staff need to review and what depends on Discord itself.

Safe boundaries

Credentials remain under your control.

Support may ask for visible context, but never for a bot token, Discord password or unrestricted server access.
